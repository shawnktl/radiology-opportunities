# AI / Imaging Informatics Fellowships

Post-residency fellowships and tracks focused on AI, machine learning, and imaging informatics for radiology trainees.

> See `../templates/opportunity-entry.md` for the entry format. Most fellowships have institution-specific application timelines; verify with each program.

### Imaging Informatics Fellowships (multiple host institutions)

- **Sponsor:** Various academic radiology departments (e.g., MGH/BWH, Stanford, UCSF, Penn, Mayo Clinic, MD Anderson, Emory, Vanderbilt, Cleveland Clinic, Duke). Programs are often listed on SIIM's fellowship directory.
- **Eligibility:** Diagnostic radiology residency graduates (post-PGY-5). Some programs accept current PGY-5 residents for the upcoming academic year.
- **Deadline:** (verify annually) — varies by program; many follow ~12–18 month lead time typical of subspecialty fellowships
- **Recurrence:** Annual at participating institutions
- **Link:** https://siim.org (SIIM maintains a fellowship listing — verify current URL)
- **Description:** One- or two-year fellowships training radiologists in PACS architecture, DICOM workflows, AI/ML deployment, and clinical informatics. Often combined with clinical subspecialty time.
- **Notes:** Some programs offer combined imaging informatics + subspecialty fellowships (e.g., body + informatics, neuro + informatics). Funding mechanisms vary.
- **Verified:** 2026-04-27

### ABPM Clinical Informatics Subspecialty Fellowship (radiologist-eligible programs)

- **Sponsor:** ACGME-accredited Clinical Informatics fellowship programs; board certification through American Board of Preventive Medicine (ABPM) or American Board of Pathology (ABP). Radiologists are eligible to pursue Clinical Informatics board certification through ABPM-affiliated radiology-hosted programs.
- **Eligibility:** Board-eligible/certified physicians, including diagnostic radiologists. Two-year ACGME-accredited fellowship.
- **Deadline:** (verify annually) — programs use individual application cycles; many post on ERAS or department sites
- **Recurrence:** Annual
- **Link:** https://www.acgme.org (search Clinical Informatics) and https://www.theabpm.org
- **Description:** ACGME-accredited subspecialty fellowship leading to Clinical Informatics board certification. Increasingly chosen by radiologists interested in enterprise IT, AI deployment, and informatics leadership roles.
- **Notes:** Distinct from Imaging Informatics fellowships, which are typically not ACGME-accredited. Clinical Informatics is the formal subspecialty board.
- **Verified:** 2026-04-27

### ACR Informatics Fellowship (Data Science Institute)

- **Sponsor:** American College of Radiology (ACR) Data Science Institute
- **Eligibility:** Fourth-year residents (R4 / PGY-5) and early-career professionals up to five years out of residency. Must be ACR-membership-eligible. Online application, CV, and one letter of recommendation required.
- **Deadline:** (verify annually) — 2026 cycle closed; selection committee notifies candidates by mid-February. Watch for the next cycle to open in late 2026.
- **Recurrence:** Annual
- **Link:** https://www.acr.org/Membership/Fellowships-and-Grants/informatics
- **Description:** Hands-on informatics fellowship pairing a trainee with the ACR Data Science Institute leadership for one-on-one mentoring and structured informatics projects. Includes attendance at ACR Annual Meetings, the SIIM-ACR Data Science Summit, and a pilot site experience week — travel and living expenses covered by ACR.
- **Notes:** Distinct from ACGME-accredited Clinical Informatics fellowships. A focused complement to research-year work or a launching pad for early-career informatics leadership roles.
- **Verified:** 2026-04-27
